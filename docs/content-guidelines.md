# Content guidelines

Editorial rules for what goes on this personal site. Aimed at anyone (human or
agent) adding talks, press, or bio copy. For *how* content is structured see
[`ARCHITECTURE.md`](../ARCHITECTURE.md); this doc covers *what's appropriate to publish*.

## Link out, don't mirror

This is a personal-branding site authored by someone who also holds a senior role
at a company (Themis AI). That overlap is fine and normal — but it has one bright
line that keeps it clean:

> **Link out to public records of work you personally did. Never re-host
> company-owned material on this domain.**

Concretely:

- ✅ **Talks you personally gave** belong here, even when the content was about a
  company product. The appearance is part of *your* professional record. Point the
  entry's `video`/`url`/`slides` at the public recording or page.
- ✅ **Press about the company** is fine as an outbound link to the outlet
  (MIT News, NVIDIA blog, etc.).
- ✅ **Self-hosting under `/files/` or `/images/` is only for assets that are
  yours to publish** — academic papers, posters, slide decks you authored and that
  are cleared for public release (e.g. the MIT-WHOI / academic PDFs).
- ❌ **Do not copy company-owned assets onto this domain** — internal or unreleased
  decks, product demos, recordings, or anything not already published by the company.
  If it isn't already public, don't be the one who publishes it here.
- ❌ **Don't link to anything that isn't already public**, even if you have access.

The test: *would this still be online if my personal site didn't exist?* If yes
(it's the company's or an outlet's public asset), link to it. If this site would be
the **origin** of the public copy, stop — that's mirroring.

## Voice & framing

- **Personal-first.** Write as "my work / my role / talks I gave," not as the
  company's official voice. Describing your title and what you build is nominative
  fair use of the company's name and marks; speaking *as* the company is not your
  call to make from here.
- **No implied endorsement.** Don't use company logos or copy in a way that suggests
  the company endorses this personal brand.
- **Keep the through-line bigger than any one employer.** Anchor the identity to the
  decade-long research thread (risk-aware, uncertainty-aware ML); the current company
  is the present chapter, not the whole frame. This also ages better across roles.

## Courtesy

When adding a batch of company-related talks/press, a one-line heads-up to whoever
owns comms/marketing at the company is cheap insurance — not permission-seeking, just
"FYI my personal site links to these public items, shout if anything's off."
