A few years ago, I published a paper called "Finding the optimal exploration-exploitation trade-off online through Bayesian risk estimation and minimization". It is 50 pages long and its main contribution is an algorithm titled "Endogenous Bayesian Regret Minimization", or EBRM, for short. While much of that complexity was required to ensure the contributions were thoroughly and properly presented, my coauthors and I all recognized that it would be a rather opaque text for most, which was a shame because it presents a solution architecture to a very real and common problem. It was one of my thesis advisors, Yogi Girdhar, who specifically suggested that I should prepare a more usable, digestible version of the paper and its contributions. I'm happy to finally have the chance to do that here, on my blog.
